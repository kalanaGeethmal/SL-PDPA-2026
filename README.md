PDPA Sri Lanka — Interactive Reference
A single-file, browser-based reference guide for Sri Lanka's Personal Data Protection Act No. 9 of 2022 and the 2025 Amendment Act No. 22.
No installation. No server. Open index.html in any modern browser.

What This Is
Sri Lanka's PDPA came into force in 2022. The 2025 amendment changed key provisions on data subject rights, cross-border transfers, and automated decision-making. This tool puts both Acts in one place, mapped to specific sections.
It is a study and reference aid. It is not legal advice.

What It Covers
Overview. Key statistics, penalty figures, and a legislative timeline from 2018 to 2025.
Controller Obligations. 12 obligations mapped to specific Act sections. Each item expands to show the full rule and an AI-specific compliance note.
Data Subject Rights. 9 rights drawn from the Act, with response timelines and 2025 changes flagged per right.
AI Systems Map. Five lifecycle stages: data sourcing, training, deployment, inference, and governance. Click a stage to see the applicable PDPA rules.
2025 Changes. A tagged log of every new provision, amendment, and removed clause from Amendment Act No. 22.
Compliance Checklist. 20 action items across four categories: Controller, Operations, DPO, and AI. Progress saves in the browser automatically.

How to Use It

Download index.html.
Open it in Chrome, Firefox, Safari, or Edge.
Use the navigation tabs to move between sections.
Tick items in the Checklist tab. Progress saves locally and persists across browser sessions.


Technical Notes

Single HTML file. All CSS and JavaScript are inline.
Fonts load from Google Fonts CDN. An internet connection is needed for correct rendering.
Checklist state saves to localStorage under the key pdpa_checklist_v1. No data leaves your browser.
No analytics. No backend. No dependencies to install.
